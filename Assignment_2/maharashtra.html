Festivals in Maharashtra

Ganesh Chaturthi

Description: Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha. It typically involves installing clay idols of Ganesha in homes and public places, offering prayers, and performing dances and songs. The festival culminates with the immersion of the idol in water.

Date: August/September (varies by year)

Significance: The festival symbolizes new beginnings, prosperity, and the removal of obstacles.

Diwali

Description: Also known as Deepavali, Diwali is the Festival of Lights. It marks the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Celebrations include lighting oil lamps (diyas), decorating homes, exchanging sweets, and bursting fireworks.

Date: October/November (varies by year)

Significance: It signifies the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana.

Makar Sankranti

Description: Makar Sankranti is a festival celebrating the harvest season and the start of longer days. It involves flying kites, feasting on special foods like tilgul (sesame seed sweets), and various cultural events.

Date: January 14

Significance: The festival marks the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makar) and is considered a time for new beginnings.
