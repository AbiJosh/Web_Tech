Festivals in West Bengal

Durga Puja

Description: Durga Puja is one of the most significant and widely celebrated festivals in West Bengal. It honors Goddess Durga's victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura. The festival features elaborate decorations, large-scale idols of Durga, cultural performances, and processions. It is celebrated with immense enthusiasm and grandeur in Kolkata and across the state.

Date: September/October (varies by year)

Significance: Durga Puja symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and is a time for communal festivities, religious rituals, and artistic displays. It marks the arrival of Goddess Durga to her maternal home and her subsequent departure.

Kali Puja

Description: Kali Puja is a major festival dedicated to Goddess Kali, celebrated primarily in West Bengal. It coincides with Diwali and involves the worship of Kali, the fierce form of Durga. The festival is marked by night-long rituals, the offering of sacrifices, and the lighting of lamps and candles. It is celebrated with special fervor in Kolkata.

Date: October/November (varies by year)

Significance: Kali Puja represents the victory of divine power over darkness and ignorance. It is observed with intense devotion and involves elaborate rituals to appease Goddess Kali and seek her blessings.

Poila Baisakh

Description: Poila Baisakh is the Bengali New Year festival celebrated on the first day of the Bengali calendar. It marks the beginning of the new year and is observed with traditional festivities, including wearing new clothes, preparing special dishes, and visiting friends and family. It is a time for starting fresh and celebrating new beginnings.

Date: April 14

Significance: Poila Baisakh signifies the start of the new year in the Bengali calendar. It is celebrated with cultural events, fairs, and family gatherings, symbolizing prosperity and good fortune for the year ahead.
