Festivals in Tamil Nadu

Pongal

Description: Pongal is a harvest festival dedicated to the Sun God. It involves cooking a special dish called Pongal, made from newly harvested rice, and offering it to the Sun God. The festival is marked by festive decorations, cultural events, and family gatherings.

Date: January 14

Significance: Pongal signifies the end of the harvest season and expresses gratitude to the Sun God for a bountiful harvest.

Diwali

Description: Also known as Deepavali, Diwali is a major Hindu festival known as the Festival of Lights. It involves lighting lamps, decorating homes, exchanging sweets, and performing prayers to celebrate the victory of light over darkness.

Date: October/November (varies by year)

Significance: Diwali marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya and is celebrated with various cultural and religious practices.

Karthigai Deepam

Description: Karthigai Deepam is a significant Tamil festival celebrated in Tamil Nadu and some other South Indian states. It involves lighting oil lamps in homes and temples to symbolize the victory of light over darkness. The festival is observed on the full moon day in the Tamil month of Karthigai.

Date: November/December (varies by year)

Significance: Karthigai Deepam is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to commemorate the lighting of the sacred flame by Lord Shiva. It is a time for family gatherings, prayers, and religious activities.
