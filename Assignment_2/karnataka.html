Festivals in Karnataka

Dussehra

Description: Dussehra, also known as Dasara, is a major Hindu festival celebrated in Karnataka. It marks the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana, as described in the epic Ramayana. The festival is celebrated with grand processions, including the famous "Jambo Savari" (elephant procession) in Mysore, where elaborate decorations and cultural performances take place.

Date: September/October (varies by year)

Significance: Dussehra symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and is observed with rituals, public celebrations, and various cultural events across the state.

Ugadi

Description: Ugadi is the New Year festival for people in Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh. It marks the beginning of the Hindu lunar calendar year. Celebrations include preparing and consuming a special dish called Ugadi Pachadi, which incorporates sweet, sour, salty, and bitter flavors, symbolizing the various experiences of life.

Date: March/April (varies by year)

Significance: Ugadi represents new beginnings and is celebrated with religious rituals, decorations, and traditional feasts, marking the start of a new year in the Hindu calendar.

Makar Sankranti

Description: Makar Sankranti is a major Hindu festival celebrated in Karnataka and other parts of India, marking the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makar). The festival is associated with harvest celebrations and involves flying kites, feasting on traditional foods like tilgul (sesame seed sweets), and various cultural activities.

Date: January 14

Significance: Makar Sankranti signifies the end of the harvest season and the beginning of longer days. It is celebrated with vibrant kite flying, special meals, and festive gatherings.
